We prove that the size of any regular resolution refutation of a Tseitin formula \(\mathrm{Ts}(G,c)\) based on a connected graph \(G=(V,E)\) is at least \(2^{\Omega(\mathrm{tw}(G)/\log |V|)}\), where \(\mathrm{tw}(G)\) denotes the treewidth of a graph \(G\). For constant-degree graphs there is known upper bound \(2^{O(\mathrm{tw}(G))}\mathrm{poly}(|V|)\) [Alekhnovich, Razborov, 2011], [Galesi, Talebanfard, Tor√°n 2020], so our lower bound is tight up to a logarithmic factor in the exponent.
